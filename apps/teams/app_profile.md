# Teams App Profile

Support meeting and chat workflows while avoiding accidental messages, calls, or notifications.

## Purpose

Teams is best for joining meetings, reviewing chats, copying meeting notes, and preparing status updates.

## Main Screens

- Activity feed
- Chat list
- Teams / channels pane
- Meeting window
- Compose editor

## Typical Tasks

- Open a specific chat or channel
- Copy meeting notes
- Draft a status update
- Join or leave a meeting

## Important Shortcuts

- `Ctrl+E`
- `Ctrl+N`
- `Ctrl+Shift+M`
- `Ctrl+Shift+O`

## Common Pitfalls

- Sending a chat message prematurely
- Posting to the wrong chat or channel
- Triggering mute or camera changes unintentionally during a meeting
- Losing focus between the main window and meeting pop-out

## Operational Notes

- Draft or send messages according to the requested endpoint
- Confirm chat or channel identity before typing
- In meetings, avoid camera, mute, or share actions unless specifically requested
- Prefer copying notes into a document editor if the task is summarization rather than posting

## Prompt Tips

- Mention the target person, chat, or channel explicitly
- State whether the result should be a copied note, a draft, or a posted message
- For meeting work, specify whether to join, observe, collect notes, or update status only
